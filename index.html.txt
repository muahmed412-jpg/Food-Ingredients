    <section id="foods">
        <h2>Popular Egyptian Foods</h2>
        <p>Egyptian cuisine blends Middle Eastern, Mediterranean, and African influences. Here are some must-try dishes:</p>
        <ul>
            <li><strong>Koshary</strong>: A hearty mix of rice, lentils, pasta, and tomato sauce, topped with fried onions and chickpeas. A national dish!</li>
            <li><strong>Falafel</strong>: Deep-fried chickpea balls, often served in pita with tahini sauce.</li>
            <li><strong>Molokhia</strong>: A green leafy stew made with jute leaves, served with rice and chicken or rabbit.</li>
            <li><strong>Fatta</strong>: Rice with bread, topped with meat sauce and vinegar – a festive dish.</li>
            <li><strong>Baklava</strong>: Sweet pastry filled with nuts and honey, a popular dessert.</li>
        </ul>
    </section>
    
    <section id="restaurants">
        <h2>Recommended Restaurants in Egypt</h2>
        <p>From Cairo to Alexandria, here are some top spots for authentic Egyptian food:</p>
        <ul>
            <li><strong>Koshary El Tahrir (Cairo)</strong>: Famous for koshary; affordable and iconic.</li>
            <li><strong>Abou El Sid (Cairo)</strong>: Known for molokhia and traditional Egyptian meals.</li>
            <li><strong>Fish Market (Alexandria)</strong>: Seafood-focused, try grilled fish with rice.</li>
            <li><strong>Cairo Kitchen (Multiple Locations)</strong>: Modern take on Egyptian classics like stuffed pigeon.</li>
            <li><strong>Sequoia (Cairo)</strong>: Upscale dining with views of the Nile, offering mezze and grills.</li>
        </ul>
        <p><em>Note</em>: Prices vary; check for halal options and local reviews on TripAdvisor or Google Maps.</p>
    </section>
    
    <section id="tips">
        <h2>Tips for Dining in Egypt</h2>
        <ul>
            <li>Visit during Ramadan for special iftar meals.</li>
            <li>Try street food in markets like Khan el-Khalili for an authentic experience.</li>
            <li>Pair meals with hibiscus tea (karkade) or mint tea.</li>
            <li>Be mindful of spice levels – ask for "shwaya" (a little) if needed.</li>
        </ul>
